Section 1

Executive Summary

SaathiPill is a medication adherence platform designed for the Indian market, targeting elderly patients and their family caregivers. The app combines manual and AI-assisted medicine entry, a multi-tier escalation reminder system, family care coordination, pharmacy refill ordering, and doctor-ready adherence reports — all operable in low-connectivity conditions.

This document catalogues every feature and screen, maps the primary user workflows, describes the core system logic, and identifies potential leakage points — moments where the system may silently fail, lose data, or allow a dose to go untracked without alerting anyone.

Scope of this document

This note covers the prototype as built in v2 of the SaathiPill HTML prototype (20 screens, 3 screen files). It is not a technical architecture document — it maps product logic and identifies UX/system gaps from a product and safety perspective.

Section 2

Application Features

2.1 Onboarding & Authentication

New users are identified via OTP-verified mobile number. The flow branches at sign-up vs. login:

  • Sign up path: Phone → OTP → Name → Onboarding cards → Profile setup → Home
  • Login path: Phone → OTP → Home (existing medicine list restored)
  • OTP is 4-digit; demo mode exposes a hint overlay for prototype testing
  • Profile setup collects name, age bracket, and primary language preference (Hindi / English)

2.2 Add Medicine Flow (5-Step Wizard)

The most critical data-entry flow. Users can enter medicines in two modes:

📸
Scan Prescription

Camera capture or gallery upload. AI reads handwritten prescriptions, auto-fills name, dose, and timing. Parsed results show 3 medicines with checkboxes to confirm/edit before saving.

⌨️
Manual Entry

5-step wizard: Medicine name → Dose → Schedule/Timing → Meal context → Priority level. Autocomplete from a 5,000+ Indian drug database. Drug interaction warning on known conflicts.

Manual Wizard — Step Detail

Step Field / Input Options / Logic
1 — Medicine Drug name search + purpose tag Autocomplete from DB; shows drug interaction warning for known pairs (e.g. Aspirin + Warfarin); purpose chips: Blood pressure, Diabetes, Cholesterol, Thyroid, Heart, Vitamin
2 — Dose Dose amount Quick chips: ½ tablet, 1 tablet, 2 tablets, 250mg, 500mg, 5ml, 10ml; free-text override
3 — Schedule Time-of-day + recurrence Preset slots: Morning (8AM), Afternoon (1PM), Evening (5PM), Night (9PM); Schedule type: Daily / Weekly / Cyclic. No custom time input currently.
4 — Meal Food relationship Before meal, After meal, Empty stomach, At bedtime
5 — Priority Criticality level Critical (alarm + call + family alert), Standard (push + louder alarm), Gentle (single push only)
Key Gap — Custom Time

Step 3 currently offers only 4 fixed time slots. Medicines prescribed at non-standard times (e.g. 6 AM insulin, 11 PM dialysis medication) cannot be accurately scheduled. A custom time picker is needed and has been flagged for inclusion.

2.3 Home Dashboard

The primary daily-use screen. Shows today's medicine schedule with per-card status — Taken, Pending, Missed, or Skipped (with reason). Key elements:

  • Adherence ring showing today's completion percentage (doctor-advised and PRN skips are excluded from the denominator)
  • Medicine cards grouped by time-of-day slot, each with name, dose, time, and meal context
  • Tap to mark taken; tap the card to view detail or open the reminder actions
  • Skipped doses render with a strike-through name, a navy "Skipped" status, and an inline reason chip (e.g. "🩺 Doctor advised · excluded from adherence") instead of appearing as Pending
  • Header summary distinguishes pending vs. skipped (e.g. "All caught up ✓ · 1 skipped") so caregivers don't chase a deliberate skip
  • Floating "Add Medicine" button always accessible
  • Quick-access tiles: AI Insights, History, Analytics, Doctor Report
  • Contextual refill warning when stock is low
  • Offline banner when device is disconnected

2.4 Reminder System

When a scheduled dose time arrives, a full-screen Reminder Modal is presented. It works on silent mode. Key elements:

  • Pulsing pill animation with medicine name, dose, time, and meal context
  • Three actions: I've taken it, Snooze 10 min, Skip this dose
  • Voice read-aloud in Hindi for accessibility
  • Escalation tier indicator shows which stage the user is at

2.5 Escalation Logic

If a reminder is not acknowledged, the system escalates through four tiers. This is the most safety-critical logic in the app:

1
Push Notification — T+0 min

Standard push notification. Works on silent mode. Patient has 10 minutes to respond.

2
Full-Screen Alarm (Louder) — T+10 min

No response within 10 minutes. Full-screen alarm overrides silent mode. Escalation indicator moves to stage 2.

3
Automated Voice Call — T+20 min

Still no response. Automated phone call in user's preferred language (Hindi/English). Call confirms dose or logs miss.

4
SMS / Family Alert — T+25 min

Caregiver (designated family member) receives an SMS plus an in-app push if they have SaathiPill installed. They can call the patient or mark it from their own device.

Escalation is per-medicine criticality

Medicines set to Gentle only receive step 1. Standard medicines escalate to step 2. Critical medicines escalate through all 4 steps. This logic must be enforced server-side, not just in the UI.

2.6 AI Insights & Tracking

✨
AI Insights

Personalised patterns detected from adherence data. Highlights best-performing times, missed-dose triggers, and suggests schedule adjustments. Adapts messaging for new vs. established users.

📅
Medication History

Calendar-style log of every taken/skipped/missed dose, filterable by medicine. Exportable. New users see an empty-state prompt to build history.

📊
Adherence Analytics

Weekly and monthly adherence graphs per medicine. Overall streak counter. Miss pattern analysis (day-of-week, time-of-day). Shared with caregiver on request.

🩺
Doctor Report

Auto-generated PDF-ready adherence report personalised to the patient's name. Includes medicines, adherence rate, missed doses, and a notes field for the doctor.

2.7 Family & Caregiver Network

The family module allows multiple caregivers to monitor one or more patients from a shared view:

  • Family Tab: Shows all family members being monitored with their today's adherence percentage, status badge (On track / Missed dose), and last-seen time
  • Caregiver View: Drill-down into a single member's today schedule, history, and streak. Caregiver can mark doses on their behalf
  • Add Family Member: Invite by mobile number via SMS link. Sets permission level (view only vs. can-mark-doses)
  • SMS plus in-app push alerts sent to caregivers on missed dose escalation (step 4)

2.8 Refills & Pharmacy

The Refills tab is a medicine order builder integrated with pharmacy channels:

  • Auto-populated with current medicine list; shows LOW stock badge for urgent items
  • Order by days supply (15/30/45/60 day buttons) or quantity (strip count)
  • Pharmacy selector: Local pharmacy, PharmEasy, 1mg
  • Delivery slot picker: Morning (9AM–12PM), Afternoon (12–5PM), Evening (5–9PM)
  • Payment options including Cash on Delivery
  • Custom item add for OTC items, specific brands, or non-medicine supplies
  • Promo code entry with auto-format and discount preview
  • Order placed state with delivery tracking entry point

2.9 Drug Interactions & Offline Mode

  • Drug Interactions Screen: Shows known interaction pairs in the user's current medicine list. Severity-coded (high / moderate / low). Links to "consult doctor" flow
  • Offline Mode: All local data (reminders, medicine list, history) remains accessible. Reminders fire normally. A persistent banner signals offline status. Saves queue locally and syncs on reconnect, with toast notification confirming sync

User Workflows

3.1 First-Time User Journey

1
Splash → Auth

User opens app. Splash screen plays. Auth screen offers Login or Sign Up.

2
OTP Verification

User enters 10-digit mobile number. Receives 4-digit OTP. Enters OTP to verify identity.

3
Onboarding Cards

3-screen swipeable intro. Explains reminders, family sharing, and refills. Skippable.

4
Profile Setup

Name, age, language preference. Optional: add a family member immediately.

5
Add First Medicine

Prompted immediately on an empty Home. 5-step wizard. Medicine saved to schedule.

6
Daily Use Loop

Home dashboard shows today's schedule. User marks doses. Reminders fire at set times. Cycle repeats.

3.2 Add Medicine Workflow (Manual)

1
Entry Mode Choice

User taps "+" → Chooser screen: Scan prescription or Type manually.

2
Medicine Name (Step 1)

Autocomplete search. Optional purpose tag. Drug interaction alert if applicable. Cannot proceed without a name.

3
Dose (Step 2)

Chip selection or free text. Cannot proceed without a dose.

4
Schedule (Step 3)

Select one or more time slots (Morning / Afternoon / Evening / Night). Choose Daily / Weekly / Cyclic. Cannot proceed without at least one time selected.

5
Meal Context (Step 4)

Before meal / After meal / Empty stomach / At bedtime. Cannot proceed without selection.

6
Priority (Step 5)

Critical / Standard / Gentle. Controls escalation depth. Default is Standard.

7
Saved → Home

Medicine added to today's schedule. Toast confirmation shown. If offline, queued and synced later.

3.3 Missed Dose Escalation Workflow

Described in detail in section 2.5. The key decision tree:

  • Dose time arrives → Notification sent
  • If taken: mark as taken, streak updated, no escalation
  • If snoozed: re-notify after 10 min, escalation timer resets
  • If skipped: logged as skip, no escalation, analytics updated
  • If no response after 10 min → escalate per criticality tier

Section 4

Potential System Leakages

A system leakage is any condition where the app silently fails to achieve its core purpose — ensuring the patient takes the right medicine at the right time — without surfacing an error or triggering a fallback. The following risks have been identified across 5 categories.

4.1 Data Entry Leakages

#Leakage PointDescriptionRisk
L-01 No custom time input Step 3 of Add Medicine offers only 4 fixed slots (8AM, 1PM, 5PM, 9PM). Medicines prescribed at non-standard times — 6AM insulin, 11PM anticoagulant — are rounded to the nearest slot, creating a timing gap that could be clinically significant. High
L-02 Scan confidence not shown The prescription scan flow shows parsed results but does not display an AI confidence score per field. Users may confirm incorrect auto-fills without realising they need to edit them. High
L-03 Purpose field not validated The medicine purpose field (e.g. "Blood pressure") is cosmetic — it does not constrain drug interaction checking or escalation logic. A mismatch between stated purpose and actual drug class could mislead caregivers. Medium
L-04 Dose unit ambiguity Free-text dose entry accepts any string. "1 tablet" and "500mg" may refer to the same physical pill, but the system stores them as separate strings. Refill order quantities derived from dose data may be incorrect. Medium
L-05 No end-date or course duration The wizard has no concept of a medicine course with an end date (e.g. antibiotics for 7 days). Medicines remain on the schedule indefinitely until manually deleted, risking continued reminders after the course completes. High

4.2 Notification & Escalation Leakages

#Leakage PointDescriptionRisk
L-06 Snooze loop without cap A user can snooze indefinitely. There is no maximum snooze count before the system escalates or logs a miss. A patient who snoozes 5 times in a row may believe they took the medicine when they did not. High
L-07 Skip vs. miss distinction unclear "Skip this dose" is a deliberate action. "No response" is a miss. Both result in a missed dose in analytics, but the cause is different — a skip may be doctor-advised, a miss may indicate a health event. The system must distinguish these in logs. Medium
L-08 Escalation reset on app kill If the escalation timer is running and the user force-quits or the OS kills the app (common on low-RAM Indian devices), the escalation chain may not resume from the correct step. Steps 3 and 4 (voice call, SMS) may never fire. High
L-09 Caregiver not notified if blocked SMS alerts at step 4 depend on the carrier delivering the message to the caregiver's handset. If the number has changed, the SIM is inactive, or the carrier filters bulk SMS as spam, the alert is silently lost without a secondary channel (in-app push for installed caregivers, voice call fallback otherwise). Medium
L-10 No reminder for next-day medicines added late If a user adds a medicine after its scheduled time today (e.g. adds an 8AM medicine at 10AM), it is unclear whether the system counts today's dose as missed or skips to tomorrow. This window is undefined. Medium

4.3 Offline & Sync Leakages

#Leakage PointDescriptionRisk
L-11 Taken marks not synced to caregiver If a patient marks a dose as taken while offline, the caregiver's view may show the dose as pending for hours (until sync). This could trigger a false family alert or unnecessary phone call. High
L-12 Conflict resolution undefined If a caregiver marks a dose as taken while the patient also marks it as taken offline, the sync may create a duplicate "taken" record or worse, overwrite one action silently. Conflict resolution strategy is not defined. Medium
L-13 Voice call escalation fails offline Steps 3 and 4 of the escalation flow require network connectivity (VoIP call, SMS gateway). If the device is offline during escalation, these steps cannot execute, and there is no local fallback. High

4.4 Family & Permission Leakages

#Leakage PointDescriptionRisk
L-14 Permission model not enforced The Add Family Member flow sets a permission level (view-only vs. can-mark-doses), but it is unclear whether the backend enforces this — or whether a view-only caregiver could mark a dose via a deep-link or URL manipulation. Medium
L-15 No patient consent for sharing A caregiver can add a patient and view all their medicines, adherence data, and history. There is no explicit consent flow on the patient's device confirming they agree to be monitored. This is a significant privacy and regulatory gap under India's DPDP Act. High
L-16 Caregiver can see drug list without context The caregiver view exposes the patient's full medicine list including sensitive medications (e.g. psychiatric, HIV, oncology drugs). No sensitivity flag or patient-controlled visibility setting exists. Medium

4.5 Refill & Drug Data Leakages

#Leakage PointDescriptionRisk
L-17 Refill quantity not tied to actual stock The refill module shows days-supply options but has no connection to actual tablet counts in the patient's possession. A patient who has 20 days remaining is not stopped from ordering 30 more days — or vice versa, may under-order. Low
L-18 Drug interaction check is entry-time only Drug interaction warnings only appear when adding a specific medicine (Aspirin trigger in demo). There is no periodic re-check as the user's medicine list grows. A new medicine added later may interact with an existing one without warning. High
L-19 Custom refill items bypass safety checks Users can add arbitrary OTC items to a refill order. No drug interaction or duplication check is performed on custom items. A user could unknowingly re-order a medicine already in their list under a brand name. Low
